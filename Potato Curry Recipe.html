Simple Aloo Sabzi Recipe in a Pressure Cooker

Ingredients

Vegetables:

  • 3 medium-sized potatoes (Aloo)
  • 1.5 medium-sized onions (Piyaaz)
  • 1 tomato (Tamatar)
  • 5 green chilies (Mirch)

Oil & Spices:

  • 1 small ladle of oil
  • 1 pinch of cumin seeds (Zeera)
  • 2 pinches of turmeric powder (Haldi)
  • 3 pinches of coriander powder (Dhaniya)
  • 2 pinches of black cumin seeds (Kalonji)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder (Lal Mirch)
  • 1 teaspoon of salt (Namak)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Cooker:
    • Add oil to the pressure cooker. Let it heat up.
    • Once hot, add 1 pinch of cumin seeds (zeera) and allow it to sizzle briefly.
  2. Sauté the Vegetables:
    • Add the onions, green chilies, and tomatoes to the cooker one by one, allowing each to cook slightly before adding the next.
    • Add salt to help the vegetables release their moisture and cook faster.
  3. Add Spices:
    • After the onions, tomatoes, and chilies soften, add turmeric (haldi) and coriander powder (dhaniya) to enhance the flavor.
    • Sauté everything until the vegetables are well-cooked.
  4. Add Potatoes and Kashmiri Chili Powder:
    • Add the potatoes to the mixture and stir well.
    • Add 1/2 tablespoon of Kashmiri chili powder for color.
  5. Cook the Potatoes:
    • Fry the potatoes until they start to turn slightly golden.
  6. Pressure Cook:
    • Add a little water, as needed, to the cooker.
    • Close the lid and let the cooker whistle 1-2 times (usually enough for the potatoes to cook through).
  7. Serve:
    • After the pressure releases, open the cooker, give the sabzi a good stir, and serve hot with roti or rice.
